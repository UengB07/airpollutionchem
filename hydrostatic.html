HYDROSTATIC EQUATION

We can derive the hydrostatic equation if we imagine a prism of air, with several forces acting on it. See above for a free body diagram, and we sum up all the necessary forces. Force one is due to the pressure on top surface. Force two is due to the weight of the object. Force three is do to the pressure on the bottom surface. Sum all the forces:

$P(dA) - (P+dP)(dA) - \rho gdz(dA) = 0$

$dP = - \rho gdz$

$dP = -\left(\frac{P}{RT}\right)gdz$

$\frac{dP}{P} = -\frac{dz}{\left(\frac{RT}{g}\right)}$

$\int{\frac{dP}{P}} = -\int{\frac{dz}{\left(\frac{RT}{g}\right)}}$

$ln(P_{z}) - ln(P_{0}) = -\frac{z-z_{0}}{\left(\frac{RT}{g}\right)}$

$ln\left(\frac{(P_{z})}{(P_{0}) }\right) = -\frac{z-z_{0}}{\left(\frac{RT}{g}\right)}$

$ln\left(\frac{(P_{z})}{(P_{0}) }\right) = -\frac{z-z_{0}}{\left(\frac{RT}{g}\right)}$

$P_{z} = P_{0}e^{-\frac{z}{\left(\frac{RT}{g}\right)}}$

We can replace the term (RT/g) with H, the scale height. H = RT/g.
