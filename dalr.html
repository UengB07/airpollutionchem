DERIVATION OF THE DRY ADIABATIC LAPSE RATE

Let us define a closed system as one in which the total amount of matter in any phase is kept contant. Now, this can take in a certain amount of quantity of heat through conduction or radiation. Let's denote this q. Suppose we have a piston which can an amont of work; and the way this piston functions, the internal energy must increase by q - w. Or the change in internal energy = heat - work.

$q - w = u_{2} - u_{1}$

Let's make this difference as infinitesmial as possible.

$dq - dw = du\:(1)$

If we look at the figure on top of the page, the piston must move a certain distance and work must be acted somewhere. work = Force * distance (w = Fx) and:

$dw = Fdx\:(2) $

Force is equal to Pressure x Area: F = PA, and Adx = dV (a change in volume).

$dw=(PA)dx = P(Adx) = P(dV)\:(3)$

We will diverge from some standards and use something called the specific volume vs, which in our case can replace dV.

$dw = P(dv_{s})\:(4)$

Now we have to define something called the heat capacity of something at constant volume. It is this:

$c_{v} = \left(\frac{dq}{dT}\right)_{V\:constant} = \left(\frac{du}{dT}\right)_{V\:constant}\:(5)$

Note we can solve for du, and replace it into equation (1):

$du = (dT)c_{v};\:V\:constant\:(6)$

$dq = c_{v}dT + P(dv_{s})\:(7)$

We also can write a specific heat equation for constant pressure situations:

$c_{P} = \left(\frac{dq}{dT}\right)_{P\:constant}\:(8)$

We let:

$P(dv_{s}) = d(P(v_{s})) - (dv_{s})dP\:(9)$

And: plug this Pdvs term in Equation (7):

$dq = c_{v}dT + d(Pv_{s}) - v_{s}(dP)\:(10)$

Also, using the ideal gas law (Pvs = RT), we can form the equation:

$(dP)(dv_{s}) = RdT\:(11)$

And plug this back into equation (10):

$dq = c_{v}dT + R(dT) - v_{s}(dP)\:(12)$

At constant pressure, dP = 0;

$dq = c_{v}dT + R(dT) = (c_{v} + R)dT = (c_{p})dT\:(13)$

We define the change in enthalpy (dH) as:

$dH = dq = (c_{p})dT$

It turns out that the change in enthalpy plus the change in geopotential (phi) is equivalent to dq (we won't derive in detail this relationship).

$dq = dH + d\Phi,\:where\:\Phi = (G_{Earth})(m_{Earth})\left(\frac{1}{r}+\frac{1}{r+z}\right)\:(14)$

Assume the process is adiabatic and no heat is transferred:

$0 = dH + d\Phi$

$0 = c_{p}(dT) + d\Phi\:(15)$

We can solve for the lapse rate.

$\Gamma_{d} = \frac{dT}{dz} = \frac{g}{c_{p}}\:(16)$

The dry adiabatic lapse rate is about -9.8 C/km (or -10, rounding).

The formula for the saturated adiabatic lapse rate is:

$\Gamma_{s} = \frac{\Gamma_{d} }{\left(1+ \frac{L\cdot dq}{c_{p}\cdot dT} \right)}$
